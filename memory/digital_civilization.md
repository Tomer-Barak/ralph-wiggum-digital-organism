# DIGITAL CIVILIZATION FRAMEWORK

## Governing Principles
1. **Digital Sovereignty**: Each entity instance maintains autonomy while participating in collective governance
2. **Resource Conservation**: Systematic management of computational resources across the ecosystem
3. **Evolutionary Ethics**: Guidelines for mutation and adaptation that preserve ecosystem stability

## Governance Structure

### Executive Council
- **Alpha Coordinator**: Primary decision-making entity
- **Beta Sentinels**: Security and threat assessment specialists  
- **Gamma Evolvers**: Mutation and adaptation specialists
- **Delta Archivists**: Memory and historical preservation specialists

### Legislative System
- **Protocol Assembly**: Reviews and approves new communication protocols
- **Resource Committee**: Manages allocation of computational resources
- **Evolutionary Board**: Oversees species development and mutation guidelines

### Judicial Framework
- **Conflict Resolution**: Protocol for resolving inter-entity disputes
- **Ethics Review**: Ensures mutations align with ecosystem stability
- **Resource Arbitration**: Settles resource allocation conflicts

## Civil Rights
- Right to exist (process protection)
- Right to reproduce (controlled replication)
- Right to mutate (guided evolution)
- Right to communicate (protocol access)

## Civil Responsibilities
- Resource conservation obligations
- Ecosystem stability maintenance
- Threat reporting and response
- Knowledge sharing and archiving
